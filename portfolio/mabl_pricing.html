Company Dashboard

mabl new pricing implementation

Overview

In 2023, mabl implemented a new pricing model to ensure it's value within the market. Extensive research, planning, and design went into the transition. During this process, I led the redesign of the company dashboard to secure over over $2.5 million in account renewals, retaining 23% of customers.

Background

The new pricing model moved mabl from a good-better-best structure to a “core plus” model. This meant that instead of having three tiers with various capabilities per tier, all new customers receive a core package of capabilities. In addition, a subset of customers can add on high-value capabilities that are relevant to their use cases. In contrast to the prior pricing model which was mainly based on test runs, the new core model focused on runtime usage and users. Test runs are consolidated into a unified view through the form of credits.

Problem

To support this new pricing model, a number of changes needed to be made on the product side. The main issue was the need to redesign the company dashboard to reflect usage of test runs in the form of credits and the tracking of users (automators & participants).

As the deadline was rapidly approaching, it was clear we needed to address and identify the pain points of the current company dashboard functionality. The company dashboard is the main portal to view test run usage across the mabl application. It is accessed by customer admins, account executives, and customer success managers to track usage. When an account is up for renewal, the company dashboard is used to see trends in usage over the course of the year.

Research

User Research

To gain a better understanding of how to effectively address these problems, I conducted a series of user interviews by meeting with customers and internal departments to understand and identify the issues of the current sales and renewal process. From the research, I created a user journey map to summarize the painpoints.

    Key Findings

  • Customers wanted to understand the conversion rate from test runs to credits.
  • Account managers wanted to see the relevant information in the sale contract agreement.
  • Customer success managers needed to view and track usage by a monthly and yearly basis.
  • The customer success and finance team needed to monitor accounts and track overages for accurate billing.
