Company Dashboard

mabl new pricing implementation

Overview

In 2023, mabl implemented a new pricing model to ensure the value of the product within the market. In this project, I led the redesign of the company dashboard. The redesign secured over 2.5 million dollars in account renewals.

Role

Lead Designer

Team

Collaborated with product manager, engineering, customer success managers, and finance team.

Duration

3 months

Final Prototype

Background

In 2023, mabl moved from a three tier pricing structure to a core package model. The three tier model limited customers to a set number of cloud runs by test type (ie. 500 Browser tests a month). Based on customer feedback, mabl implemented a new flexible package model which provided companies with an annual or monthly allowance of "credits" which could be used across all test types.

Problem

Tracking test run usage across accounts and workspaces posed a challenge for teams. Customers and internal teams struggled to calculate yearly and monthly usage to determine proper size of a new sales agreement or account renewal.

Objective

Research, strategize, and redesign the company dashboard to align with the new pricing model and make it simple for teams to track test run usage and active users across their workspaces.

Research

User Research

The company dashboard is the main portal to track test run usage in the application. To identify and address the pain points of the current dashboard functionality, I conducted a series of user interviews with customers and internal departments and summarized my findings in a user journey map.

    Key Findings

  • Customer success managers needed to view and track usage by a monthly and yearly basis.
  • Customers wanted to understand the conversion rate from test runs to credits.
  • Account managers wanted to see the relevant information in the sale contract agreement.
  • Customer success and finance team needed to monitor accounts and track overages for accurate billing.

Wireframe

In the early phases of wireframing, I detected crucial components lacking in both the information layout and functionality.

Designing for accounts

Through my research, I discovered that enterprise companies frequently utilize numerous accounts, each containing several workspaces. Consequently, I designed an accounts page to streamline the organization of these workspaces.

Track usage monthly or yearly basis

On the workspace page, I redesigned the top filter to allow users to switch between an annual or monthly view.

Credit to test run conversion

Help customers understand the conversion rate from test runs to credits.

Track overages

Display account and workspace overages.

Result

The company launched its new dashboard in January 2024. About 15% of existing accounts transitioned to the new pricing model. This redesign contributed to securing over $2.5 million in account renewals. Users quickly appreciated the insights it provided into test run usage and active users across workspaces. Furthermore, the customer success and finance teams were pleased with the dashboard's ability to help them efficiently assess the size and scope of new contracts.
